Kolkata Tourist Places

Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial is a large marble building in Kolkata, West Bengal, India, which was built between 1906 and 1921. It is dedicated to the memory of Queen Victoria and is now a museum and tourist destination.

Howrah Bridge

The Howrah Bridge is a famous cantilever bridge over the Hooghly River in Kolkata. It is one of the busiest bridges in the world and an iconic symbol of Kolkata.

New Town EcoPark

New Town Eco Park in Kolkata offers 480 acres of eco-friendly urban space with diverse zones, boating, butterfly garden, amphitheater, and cultural events, promoting sustainability and recreational activities.

Dakshineshwar Kali Temple

Dakshineshwar Kali Temple in Kolkata is a renowned Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Kali. It attracts pilgrims and tourists for its architectural beauty, spiritual significance, and religious festivals.

Fort William

Fort William in Kolkata is a historic military fort built during British rule. It offers tours showcasing its architectural heritage, military history, and lush grounds, attracting history enthusiasts and tourists.

Marble Place

Marble Palace in Kolkata is a majestic mansion showcasing European architecture, marble interiors, and an impressive art collection. It offers guided tours, drawing art enthusiasts and history buffs alike.
